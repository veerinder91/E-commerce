Growing health issues in the youth!

Let’s take a moment to talk about something we usually ignore — the health problems young people are facing today. On the outside, they look full of energy, dreams, and big plans. But if we look a little closer, we’ll see that many young people are quietly struggling with serious health issues. The scary part? Most of them don’t even realize it until it becomes a big problem.

The New Normal: Stress, Anxiety, and Depression

Before, only older people used to talk about stress. But now, even young students and workers feel stressed every day. They have to deal with exams, job pressure, social media, and personal problems all at once. That’s why more young people feel sad, worried, and tired these days.

Have you seen someone say, “I’m just tired,” or “I’m fine,” but their smile looks fake? Many times, they are not really okay. They might be having trouble sleeping, feeling scared, or feeling like life is too hard.

The Silent Attack: Lifestyle Diseases

Before, people thought diseases like diabetes, high blood pressure, and obesity only happened to older people. But now, even teenagers and young adults are getting these problems.

The reasons are bad eating habits, not doing exercise, and spending too much time on phones and computers.

Think about it — how many times do we eat chips instead of fruits? Or watch shows late at night until 3 a.m.? It feels fun now, but slowly, it is making our health worse.

Body Image Struggles and Eating Disorders

Today, because of apps like Instagram and TikTok, many young people feel pressure to look “perfect.” They think they are not good enough if they don’t look a certain way.

This wrong thinking has caused more problems like eating too little, eating too much, or making themselves sick after eating.

Imagine feeling every day that your body is not good enough. It’s very sad, and it is happening to many young people.

Substance Abuse

Another big problem is that more young people are using alcohol, tobacco, and drugs. Many start because they want to forget their problems, because of pressure from friends, or just to fit in.

They think it’s only “one time,” but soon it can become a very dangerous habit.

What Can We Do About It?

First, we need to talk openly and honestly. Being healthy is not just about having a good-looking body; it’s about feeling good in your mind, heart, and body.

Second, we can make small, simple changes:

Go for a short walk every day instead of spending hours on the phone. Talk to friends for real instead of just liking their pictures. Eat carefully and choose healthy food instead of eating junk without thinking. Ask for help when you feel stressed, and listen kindly when someone else needs help.

Being young should be the best time of our lives. We should not let health problems take away our happiness.We must take care of our health now, not just for today, but for our future too.

Let’s start today — with one small step at a time.
